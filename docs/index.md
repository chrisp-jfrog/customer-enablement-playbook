    <h2>The Work</h2>
  <p>
    I sit at the intersection of technical depth and customer empathy. My role is to understand what a customer is trying to build, translate that into a working architecture inside our product, and then make sure their team has everything they need to operate it independently.
  </p>
  <p>
    That means getting into the weeds on API integrations, SSO configurations, and data models — and also running executive business reviews, writing escalation runbooks, and designing onboarding programs that scale across a CSM team of twelve.
  </p>

  <h3>What I Focus On</h3>
  <p>
    Most customer churn is silent and predictable. It shows up as low adoption in week three, an unresolved integration ticket in week five, and a missed renewal in month eleven. My work is to interrupt that pattern before it starts — with structured onboarding, proactive health signals, and content that answers the question before the customer thinks to ask it.
  </p>

  <h2>Background</h2>
  <p>
    I came to Customer Success from a Solutions Engineering background, which means I can hold a technical conversation with a customer's infrastructure team in the morning and present a success plan to their CFO in the afternoon. I've worked with customers ranging from early-stage startups moving fast to global enterprises with complex compliance requirements and multi-region deployments.
  </p>
  <p>
    Over time, I started noticing that the best customer outcomes weren't driven by heroic individual effort — they were driven by systems. A well-designed onboarding checklist consistently outperforms the best CSM improvising. That insight is what led me to build the Customer Enablement Playbook.
  </p>

  <h3>Technical Depth</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>REST and GraphQL API integration patterns</li>
    <li>SSO / SAML / OAuth configuration and troubleshooting</li>
    <li>Data pipeline architecture for enterprise customers</li>
    <li>Webhook design and event-driven workflow setup</li>
    <li>Role-based access control and multi-tenant architecture</li>
    <li>CRM integration (Salesforce, HubSpot) and data sync</li>
  </ul>

  <h3>Enablement & CS Skills</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>Onboarding program design and 30/60/90-day success planning</li>
    <li>Escalation runbook authoring and incident management</li>
    <li>QBR facilitation and executive stakeholder communication</li>
    <li>CSM enablement: playbooks, training, ramp programs</li>
    <li>Health scoring models and at-risk account identification</li>
    <li>Cross-functional alignment between CS, Product, and Engineering</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Philosophy</h2>
  <p>
    The best customer relationships are built on clarity. Customers don't need to be delighted at every touchpoint — they need to trust that when something breaks, someone will pick up the phone; that the person they're talking to actually understands their stack; and that the product will do what it was sold to do.
  </p>
  <p>
    My job is to make that trust structural, not personal. When I leave an account, the next CSM should be able to pick up the playbook and run.
  </p>
